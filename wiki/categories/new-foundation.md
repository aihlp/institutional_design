# Category: New Empirical Foundation

## Overview

This category documents the proposed alternative framework that redefines institutions as **behavioral clusters** rather than rules, norms, or shared beliefs.

## Core Proposal

**New Definition**: Institutions are empirically distinguishable, statistically stable clusters of behavioral trajectories that arise from a spectrum of possible reactions to an identifiable informational signal.

## What Changes

| From (Classical) | To (New Framework) |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Rules (unobservable constraints) | Behavioral clusters (observable patterns) |
| Norms (shared expectations) | Statistical groupings |
| Shared beliefs (mental states) | Trajectory similarities |
| Inferred from behavior | Identified in behavior |
| Ontological discovery | Methodological construction |

## What Stays the Same

✅ Core observation: Collective behavior exhibits stable, self-reinforcing patterns

✅ Explanatory ambition: Account for how institutions shape action

✅ Cumulative research program: Build knowledge across studies

## The Three-Tier Architecture

### [[Tier 1: Base Definitions]]
Primitives directly translatable to digital traces:
- Informational Signal
- Reaction
- Behavioral Trajectory
- Behavioral Cluster
- Behavioral Spectrum

### [[Tier 2: Theoretical Scaffold]]
Explanatory mechanisms:
- Perception filters
- Habituation and social learning
- Opportunism (Williamson, 1985)
- Signal-to-norm dynamics

### [[Tier 3: Mathematical Operationalization]]
Computational toolkit:
- Trajectory clustering (UMAP, HDBSCAN)
- Stationarity testing (ADF, KPSS)
- Transition matrices (Markov chains)
- Network contagion models

## Philosophical Foundation

[[Rapoport's Subjectivism]] provides the methodological basis:
- Institutions are constructed by the analyst, not discovered in nature
- Analyst's criteria are explicit choices, not hidden assumptions
- Operationalization is the primary conceptual act

## Advantages Over Classical Framework

1. **Observable**: No inference about mental states required
2. **Measurable**: Uses standard statistical tools
3. **Reproducible**: Explicit criteria enable replication
4. **Falsifiable**: Claims can be empirically tested
5. **Cumulative**: Results build across studies
