# Algorithmic Exclusion

## Summary

Platform algorithms systematically exclude demographic groups from informational signals through optimization processes that no human designed, no rule authorized, and no oversight mechanism detected.

## The Foundational Study

**Lambrecht and Tucker (2018)** demonstrated:
- Women significantly less likely to be targeted by STEM education ads
- **Not due to explicit policy**
- **Cause**: Purely algorithmic optimization for cost efficiency

### The Mechanism

```
Women are more expensive to target on social media
→ Algorithm maximizes clicks per dollar
→ Preferentially delivers STEM ads to men
→ Result: Gender-differentiated information environment
```

## Broader Context

**Mehrabi et al. (2022)** documented:
- Bias and fairness in machine learning
- Algorithmic systems encode and amplify social inequalities
- Mechanisms invisible to users and regulators

**UBC Sauder School of Business (2025)**:
- Brought findings to wider public attention
- Entire demographic structurally excluded from educational opportunity
- No human designed, authorized, or detected the exclusion

## Why This Breaks Classical Institutional Theory

### No Rule, No Norm, No Shared Belief

| Classical Requirement | Algorithmic Reality |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| Formal rule states exclusion | ❌ No such rule exists |
| Cultural norm prescribes it | ❌ No such norm exists |
| Enforcement mechanism sanctions it | ❌ No such mechanism exists |
| **Yet stable pattern emerges** | ✅ High statistical reliability |

### An Institution No One Chose

This is an institution that:
- ✅ Meets functional criteria of institutional persistence (stable, shapes action, self-reinforcing)
- ❌ Lacks every feature classical theory identifies as constitutive (no rule, no norm, no shared belief)

**Generated by**: Algorithmic filter, not rule-following behavior

## Implications

If institutional theory cannot accommodate this phenomenon without stretching definitions beyond recognition:
- **The definitions themselves must be revised**, not the phenomenon explained away

This represents a category of institutions that:
1. Exist without human intention
2. Operate without normative content
3. Persist without explicit enforcement
4. Are measurable only through behavioral patterns
