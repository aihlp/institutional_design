# Scott's Three Pillars

## Definition

**W. Richard Scott** (2014): Institutions comprise three pillars that operate together to produce stable social patterns.

*Source: Institutions and Organizations (3rd edition)*

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Regulative Pillar
- **Focus**: Formal rules and sanctions
- **Mechanism**: Coercive enforcement
- **Examples**: Laws, regulations, contracts, policies
- **Logic**: Expedience (actors comply to avoid punishment)

### 2. Normative Pillar
- **Focus**: Shared expectations and obligations
- **Mechanism**: Social pressure and moral governance
- **Examples**: Professional standards, codes of conduct, role expectations
- **Logic**: Social obligation (actors comply because they should)

### 3. Cultural-Cognitive Pillar
- **Focus**: Shared frames of meaning and taken-for-granted assumptions
- **Mechanism**: Mimetic processes, internalized schemas
- **Examples**: Cultural narratives, cognitive categories, unquestioned practices
- **Logic**: Orthodoxy (actors comply because it's "the way things are done")

## Advance Over North

Scott's framework captured something North's model missed:
- Institutions operate not only through explicit incentives
- Background assumptions make certain behaviors seem natural, obvious, or unthinkable
- Recognizes multiple mechanisms of institutional influence

## Critical Limitations

### The Same Operationalization Problem

The cultural-cognitive pillar in particular **resists operationalization**:
- How does one measure a "taken-for-granted assumption"?
- Scott acknowledged the three pillars are not always empirically distinguishable
- Their relative importance varies across contexts

### Descriptive vs. Identificatory

The framework provides:
- ✅ A vocabulary for **describing** institutions after identification
- ❌ No procedure for **identifying** them in the first place

## Related Work

### Aoki (2007) Synthesis
"Institutions are self-sustaining, salient patterns of social interaction represented by the shared beliefs of the players."

**Advance**: Shifted ontological status from external constraints to emergent equilibria

**Problem**: Relocated measurement problem rather than solving it—now must measure "shared beliefs," equally unobservable

### Weik and Walgenbach (2021)
- Proposed latent-variable measurement model for institutional constructs
- **Result**: Latent structures cannot be recovered from observable data without fundamental reorientation

### Hindriks (2022)
- Recent synthesis of rules-versus-equilibria debate
- Confirms shared measurement problem on both sides
