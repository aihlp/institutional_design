# Tier 2: Theoretical Scaffold

## Overview

This tier connects the base definitions from Tier 1 to established theoretical frameworks, creating a bridge between empirical primitives and mathematical formalization.

## Core Functions

### Theoretical Integration

Tier 2 serves as the conceptual scaffold that:
- Links behavioral primitives to institutional theory
- Explains mechanisms connecting signals to outcomes
- Provides causal narratives for observed patterns

### Key Mechanisms

1. **Signal Processing** - How users receive and interpret informational signals
2. **Behavioral Adaptation** - Process of trajectory formation through repeated reactions
3. **Cluster Formation** - Emergence of behavioral groupings from individual trajectories
4. **Institutional Identification** - Recognition of stable patterns as institutions

## Relationship to Other Tiers

- **From Tier 1**: Uses the five primitives as building blocks
- **To Tier 3**: Provides conceptual structure for mathematical modeling
