# Ethics & Governance Framework

## Institutional Design Lab Ethics Principles

This repository operates as an open research laboratory for studying institutional dynamics in digital systems. Our ethical framework ensures responsible research practices that prioritize transparency, democratic values, and public benefit.

---

## Core Ethical Commitments

### 1. Transparency

- All research methods, data sources, and analytical procedures are documented openly
- Simulation models and forecasting systems include clear documentation of assumptions and limitations
- Research outputs distinguish between empirical findings, theoretical claims, and speculative projections

### 2. Anti-Authoritarian Safeguards

This research infrastructure is explicitly designed to:

- **Prevent concentration of power**: Tools and insights are made publicly accessible to avoid monopolization by powerful actors
- **Support democratic governance**: Research prioritizes applications that strengthen participatory decision-making
- **Enable accountability**: Measurement systems are designed to hold institutions accountable, not to enable surveillance or control

### 3. Misuse Prevention

We acknowledge risks of misuse and implement safeguards:

- **Dual-use awareness**: Institutional measurement tools could theoretically be used for manipulation; we prioritize defensive applications
- **Access considerations**: While open, documentation emphasizes responsible use cases
- **Community oversight**: Governance mechanisms allow community input on controversial applications

### 4. Responsible Experimentation

When conducting simulations or interventions:

- **No real-world harm**: Simulations are clearly distinguished from live interventions
- **Informed consent**: Any human subjects research follows established ethical review processes
- **Proportionality**: Intervention studies consider potential downstream effects

### 5. Democratic Safeguards

Research priorities and applications should:

- Strengthen pluralistic governance arrangements
- Enable diverse stakeholder participation
- Support institutional designs that distribute rather than concentrate authority
- Prioritize vulnerable populations in impact assessments

### 6. Data Privacy Principles

When working with behavioral data:

- **Minimization**: Collect only data necessary for research objectives
- **Anonymization**: Remove personally identifiable information where possible
- **Purpose limitation**: Use data only for stated research purposes
- **Security**: Implement appropriate technical safeguards for stored data
- **Compliance**: Respect applicable data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

---

## Prohibited Uses

This research infrastructure should NOT be used for:

1. **Manipulative governance**: Designing institutions to manipulate rather than empower participants
2. **Surveillance enhancement**: Using behavioral clustering for invasive monitoring
3. **Authoritarian optimization**: Helping authoritarian regimes strengthen control mechanisms
4. **Discriminatory targeting**: Using institutional analysis to enable discriminatory practices
5. **Undue influence**: covertly shaping behavior without transparency or consent

---

## Responsible Application Domains

Recommended uses include:

- **Platform accountability**: Measuring and reporting on platform governance effects
- **Democratic innovation**: Designing more participatory governance mechanisms
- **Regulatory analysis**: Supporting evidence-based policy development
- **Academic research**: Advancing understanding of institutional dynamics
- **Public interest advocacy**: Enabling civil society monitoring of institutional change

---

## Review Process

Ethics questions should be raised through:

1. GitHub Issues tagged `ethics`
2. Discussion in governance forums
3. Direct contact with maintainers for urgent concerns

Significant ethical concerns trigger:

- Community discussion period
- Maintainer review
- Potential modification or restriction of affected components
