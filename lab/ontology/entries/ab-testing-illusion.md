# A/B Testing Illusion

## Summary

Platform A/B testing infrastructure systematically violates the random assignment principle that experimental science depends on, revealing a fundamental fracture in classical institutional theory.

## The Core Problem

**Cornil and Hardisty (2025)** - Systematic review in *International Journal of Research in Marketing*:
- Examined all known peer-reviewed studies using Meta and Google A/B testing tools
- **Finding**: No real random assignment exists

### Why Random Assignment Collapses

```
Classical Assumption: Users randomly assigned to treatment variants
Platform Reality: ML algorithms select users based on predicted click-through probability
Result: Treatment effects are confounded by algorithmic selection
```

**Quote**: "Even if we asked people at Facebook, 'Why was this group of people selected?', they wouldn't know the answer." (EurekAlert, 2025)

## Scale of the Problem

- Every Facebook user is an unwitting participant in ~10 simultaneous experiments
- Billions of behavioral observations generated under non-experimental conditions
- Institutional theory cannot account for conditions under which data are produced

## Supporting Evidence

### Goli et al. (2022)
- **Interference bias** in platform ranking experiments
- Assumption of independent treatment effects systematically violated
- Algorithmic environments invalidate classical experimental design

### Braun and Schwartz (2022)
- Advertising contexts specifically
- Algorithmic targeting confounds A/B test results
- Measured treatment effects unreliable as indicators of true causal impact

### Wu et al. (2023)
- **Non-stationarities** in platform metrics introduce bias over time
- Behavioral data not stable measurements they appear to be
- Even properly designed experiments produce biased results

## Implications for Institutional Theory

### Signal and Filter Are Not Independent

| Classical View | Platform Reality |
|----------------|------------------|
| Signal = ad creative, interface, policy notification | Signal + Filter = entangled system |
| Filter = neutral medium | Filter = active, opaque agent |
| Environment = stable backdrop | Environment = dynamic, self-modifying |

### Neither North Nor Scott Can Account For This

- **North's "rules of the game"**: Assumes stable rules, not adaptive systems
- **Scott's "cultural-cognitive pillar"**: Assumes human-readable norms, not algorithmic processes
- **Both assume**: Informational environment is stable backdrop
- **Reality**: Environment is dynamic, self-modifying, partially unobservable

## The Devastating Conclusion

If institutional theory cannot account for the experimental conditions under which billions of behavioral observations are generated:
- The scope of explanatory claims must be radically circumscribed
- Data exist in abundance
- Conceptual tools to interpret them do not
