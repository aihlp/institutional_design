# Institutional Theory Wiki

## Overview

This wiki documents the **measurement crisis in institutional theory** and proposes a **new empirical foundation** based on behavioral clusters rather than rules, norms, or shared beliefs.

## The Core Problem

Institutional theory faces an uncomfortable irony: the discipline that set out to explain how collective behavior becomes stable and predictable now finds itself **unable to measure its own central object**.

Classical definitions (North's "rules of the game," Scott's "three pillars") define institutions in terms of **latent constructs** that cannot be observed in behavioral trace data. This is not a minor methodological inconvenience—it is a structural failure that has been building for three decades.

## The Proposed Solution

**New Definition**: Institutions are empirically distinguishable, statistically stable clusters of behavioral trajectories that arise from reactions to identifiable informational signals.

This reorientation:
- Transforms institutional analysis from interpretative enterprise to empirical science
- Uses data that digital platforms already collect
- Enables reproducible, falsifiable research
- Handles phenomena classical theory cannot explain (algorithmic exclusion, platform governance)
