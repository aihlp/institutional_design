# Classical Institutional Theory

## Overview

This category contains entries related to the classical definitions and frameworks of institutional theory that have structured the field for over three decades.

## Core Problem

Classical institutional theory defines institutions in terms of **latent constructs** (rules, norms, constraints, shared mental models) that are **structurally non-operationalizable**—they cannot be directly observed or measured in behavioral trace data.

## Research Questions

Classical theory leaves these questions unanswered:
1. How do we measure a "rule" that exists only as a mental construct?
2. How do we identify a "norm" without circular inference from behavior?
3. How do we distinguish between competing definitions empirically?
4. How do we account for institutions that emerge without human intention?
