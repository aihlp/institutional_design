# Douglass North

## Overview

Douglass North (1920-2015) was a Nobel Prize-winning economist and one of the most influential scholars in institutional theory. His work forms a cornerstone of the classical institutional framework.

## Key Contributions

### Definition of Institutions

North's famous definition frames institutions as:
> "The rules of the game in a society or, more formally, ... the humanly devised constraints that shape human interaction."

This rule-based conception has dominated institutional economics for decades.

### Major Works

- **North, D. C. (1990).** *Institutions, Institutional Change and Economic Performance*
- **North, D. C. (1991).** "Institutions" - Journal of Economic Perspectives
- **North, D. C., Wallis, J. J., & Weingast, B. R. (2009).** *Violence and Social Orders*

## Relevance to Behavioral Cluster Framework

North's work represents the classical approach that the behavioral cluster framework seeks to revise. While North emphasized rules and formal constraints, the behavioral cluster approach focuses on empirically observable patterns of behavior.

### Critique from New Foundation

The behavioral cluster framework argues that:
- Rules alone cannot explain observed behavioral variation
- Formal constraints may not capture actual behavioral patterns
- Empirical measurement should focus on behaviors, not stated rules
