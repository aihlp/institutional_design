# Hodgson 2015

## Citation

Hodgson, G. M. (2015). "What are institutions?" *Journal of Economic Issues*, 40(1), 1-25.

## Summary

This influential article examines the nature and definition of institutions in economic and social theory. Hodgson provides a comprehensive analysis of institutional definitions and argues for a clear conceptual framework.

## Key Arguments

### Definition Criteria

Hodgson proposes that adequate institutional definitions should:
- Distinguish institutions from organizations
- Account for both formal and informal constraints
- Recognize the role of shared understandings
- Enable empirical operationalization

### Critique of Existing Definitions

The article critically evaluates definitions from:
- North's rule-based approach
- Scott's multi-pillar framework
- Various game-theoretic formulations

## Relevance to Behavioral Cluster Framework

Hodgson's rigorous examination of institutional definitions supports the behavioral cluster framework's call for:
- Clear operational definitions
- Empirical measurability
- Distinction between patterns and prescriptions

### Points of Agreement

- Need for precise institutional concepts
- Importance of empirical grounding
- Recognition of informal dimensions

### Points of Departure

The behavioral cluster framework extends Hodgson's analysis by:
- Focusing on observed behavioral patterns rather than conceptual definitions
- Using clustering algorithms for institutional identification
- Emphasizing temporal dynamics over static categorization
